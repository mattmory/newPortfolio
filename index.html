About

My first job out of college was as a computer systems validation analyst, a fancy way to say tester, for quality systems in the pharmaceutical industry. Over the next few years I wore many hats: business analyst, systems analyst, eventually becoming the global technical owner of a global quality management system based on the TrackWise platform. I was responsible for the system's day to day operation, all upgrades and enhancements, change management, end-user support and anything else you could think of. With 10,000 users over a dozen timezones it was a lot of meetings.

I learned a lot in that role but I wasn't getting my hands as dirty as I once was. A move from Philadelphia to Phoenix gave me the chance to get back into the development world by enrolling in The University of Arizona's Full Stack Web Development Bootcamp.

My favorite part of development is interacting with existing APIs and also creating my own APIs. Bringing together multiple datasets for display as usable information is a puzzle that I never get tired of solving.

Publications

During my senior year of college my school's Astrophysics lab was looking for someone with Linux experience to get the lab's computers up and running. My advisor recommended me for the job and what was supposed to be a quick job building a few workstations and knowledge transfer turned into a yearlong internship as a research assistant.

In addition to Linux administration I wrote a great deal of applications, mostly in C, to process data from radio and X-ray telescopes into usable information. Additional applications were created to transform this data into graphs and figures suitable for publication using the SuperMongo libraries.

PDFs of the publications are available at the following links:
  • A Detailed Study of the Lobes of Eleven Powerful Radio Galaxies
  • Improved Constraints on the Acceleration History of the Universe and the Properties of the Dark Energy
  • Cosmological Studies with Radio Galaxies and Supernovae
  • The Properties and Gaseous Environments of Powerful Classical Double Radio Galaxies
  • Radio Galaxy Redshift-Angular Size Data Constraints on Dark Energy
  • High-Redshift Radio Galaxies as a Cosmological Tool: Exploration of a Key Assumption and Comparison with Supernova Results
